An **OWD app** is not a standalone Nuxt page: it is a **publishable Nuxt module** that, when listed in **`desktop.config.ts`**, registers one or more **desktop programs** (launcher entries, windows, commands).

## What an app provides

| Concept | Role |
|---------|------|
| **Nuxt module** (`defineNuxtModule`) | Adds components, plugins, Tailwind paths, optional `runtimeConfig`. |
| **Runtime plugin** | Usually on `app:created` calls **`defineDesktopApp(...)`** with the app configuration. |
| **`ApplicationConfig`** | Stable id, title, icons, **windows** (models), **entries** (menu items), **commands** (actions). |
| **Build** (`@nuxt/module-builder`) | Outputs `dist/module.mjs` consumed by the desktop like any other Nuxt module. |

## What it should *not* do

- Do not depend on a specific theme (Win95, etc.): depend only on **`@owdproject/core`** as a peer and its public APIs.
- Do not assume fixed host URL routes: the app runs **inside** the desktop (windows, not necessarily global router paths).
